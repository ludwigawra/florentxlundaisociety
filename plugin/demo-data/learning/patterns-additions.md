
### Decide-by-date beats decide-later
**When**: A choice is surfaced that has more than one good option and no forced deadline.
**Do**: Attach an explicit decide-by date, even a soft one. The absence of a date is how reversible decisions become permanent by default.
**Why**: Two decisions in the last 30 days sat for 3+ weeks without action. In both cases the "wait and see" position was actually a choice — and it was the wrong one.
**Source**: Extracted from pricing and hiring-track decisions, April 2026.

### One real user beats ten interviews
**When**: Deciding whether to keep building in a vacuum or put what exists in front of someone.
**Do**: Ship the smaller thing to one real user. Interview-only feedback loops drift toward what people imagine they want.
**Why**: The v1 timeline decision made this explicit — shipping beats more speccing.
**Source**: Extracted from [[ship-v1-end-of-month]].
